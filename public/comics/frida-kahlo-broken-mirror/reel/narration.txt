Frida Kahlo was eighteen when a crash split her life in two.

Before the bus and trolley collided, she planned on medicine, not mythology.

The accident shattered her body and forced a long recovery.

Confined to bed, she faced a mirror and began turning pain into image.

Her self-portraits did not soften suffering. They made it impossible to look away.

From a narrow room came a public language of identity, disability, and survival.

Her body kept arguing with disappearance. The paintings argued back.

The crash broke the plan. The mirror helped build the work that remains.
